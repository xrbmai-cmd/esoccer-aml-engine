Two honest limits, not a perfect score. Naive single-signal rules flag every shared-IP household and land at ~4% precision. Scoring the full combination lifts that to 69% at 72% recall, clearing 5,199 of 5,220 legitimate accounts — but it catches the obvious rings and misses the stealth ones (small, uneven, slow washes that sit under the threshold). And precision caps here: a residual set of legitimate bettors place coincidental large opposing co-bets that are behaviourally indistinguishable from washing on transaction data alone. Closing either gap costs the other — tighter rules miss more stealth, looser rules flood analysts. Real resolution needs context behaviour can't see: KYC, source-of-funds, cross-book history.
